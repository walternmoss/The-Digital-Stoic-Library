The Digital Stoic Library

A textual resource for Stoic studies

Welcome to a specialized digital resource for the study of Stoicism. This project provides technically rigorous, lemma-tagged editions of Epictetus’s Enchiridion and Marcus Aurelius’s Meditations.

Unlike standard translations, this library is built upon a Master Stoic Lexicon of key technical terms. Using advanced computational linguistics, we have mapped every occurrence of these terms in the original Ancient Greek back to their English counterparts, allowing students and scholars to see the "Logos" behind the translation.

The Enchiridion

Instructional and imperative. Epictetus' handbook of Stoic practice as recorded by Arrian.

The Meditations

Personal and cosmic. The private reflections of the great Stoic Emperor, Marcus Aurelius.

User Guide & Navigation

  • Thematic Filter: Use the dropdown menu at the top to filter the text by technical concepts. The chapter list will automatically update to show only relevant passages.
  • Interactive Tagging: Click the Lexical Tags at the bottom of the Greek pane to "pin" a concept. The reader will instantly highlight all inflected forms of that Greek word in the original text.
  • Scholarly Notes: The bottom pane provides thematic commentary focused on the technical application of the Stoic lexicon within each specific passage.
